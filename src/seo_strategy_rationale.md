# SEO Strategy & Rationale: naveengaur.com

This document serves as the "Source of Truth" for our SEO and AEO strategy. It explains **why** we are building certain content and **how** it maps to real user demand.

---

## 1. Core Growth Pillars (The "Goldmines")
Based on Google Ads data, we have identified two high-volume pillars (1K – 10K monthly searches) that will drive the majority of our organic traffic.

### Pillar A: Security & Malware Recovery
*   **Primary Keywords:** `wordpress malware cleanup` (1K-10K), `wordpress malware removal service` (100-1K).
*   **Rationale:** This is our highest-volume category. Users searching for this are in a "crisis state" and have a very high intent to hire a professional immediately.
*   **Action:** Build the most comprehensive "Malware Cleanup" guide on the web.

### Pillar C: Managed Maintenance & Support
*   **Primary Keywords:** `wordpress maintenance service` (1K-10K), `wordpress website maintenance` (1K-10K), `wordpress support plans` (100-1K).
*   **Rationale:** Targeting the "Ready-to-Hire" buyer. These users are looking for a professional "service" or "plan," not a DIY tutorial.
*   **Action:** Build a high-conversion "Maintenance Plans" page using "Service-Intent" keywords.

### Pillar D: WordPress Migration (The "Hook")
*   **Primary Keywords:** `wordpress migration` (1K-10K), `transfer wordpress site` (1K-10K), `shopify to wordpress migration` (100-1K).
*   **Rationale:** High-intent frustration/emergency search. These jobs are entry points that lead directly to maintenance retainers.
*   **Action:** Create a dedicated landing page (NOT homepage lead) for migration services.

---

## 3. The "White-Label" Agency Strategy
This targets a completely different buyer: **Agencies** looking for overflow support.

*   **Primary Keywords:** `whitelabel wordpress maintenance` (100-1K, +900% growth), `white label wordpress developer`.
*   **Rationale:** Agencies evaluate risk and reliability, not just speed. They need a "silent partner."
*   **Tone:** Professional, process-oriented, risk-mitigating.
*   **Key Messaging:** "Silent extension of your team," "NDA-ready," "Documented SOPs," "Reliable SLAs."
*   **Action:** Build a dedicated `/agency` or `/white-label` page with a formal inquiry form.

---

## 4. Architecture: Homepage vs. Landing Pages
To avoid diluting our SEO and user conversion, we follow this architectural split:

1.  **Homepage:** Stays focused on **"Panic + Emergency Fixes"** (Crashes, Hacks, Downtime). This is the strongest hook for cold traffic.
2.  **Dedicated Landing Pages:** Used for specialized, high-volume searches that aren't "emergencies" (e.g., `/migration`, `/woocommerce`, `/agency`). This keeps the homepage message clean and high-impact.

## 4. Content Rationale (Reasoning)

1.  **Why write about "Plugin Crashes" if volume is 0-10?**
    *   *Reason:* It builds "Topical Authority." Google sees that you don't just talk about broad topics, but you understand the "guts" of WordPress. It also feeds AI models with specific solutions.
2.  **Why focus on "Malware Cleanup" over "General Development"?**
    *   *Reason:* 10K searches vs. 10 searches. Malware cleanup is a "burning house" problem. People don't shop around for weeks; they hire the first expert who looks competent.
3.  **Why target "WooCommerce Speed" specifically?**
    *   *Reason:* WooCommerce clients are usually making money. A slow site costs them *actual dollars*. They are the highest-paying demographic for speed services.
