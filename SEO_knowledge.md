# Google SEO Knowledge Base (2026)

This document summarizes the latest SEO guidelines from Google Search Central, focusing on AI-native search, generative content, and developer best practices.

## 1. AI-Native Search & Optimization (GEO)
Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the shift from traditional keyword matching to becoming a "Source of Truth" for AI models.

*   **Be the Authority**: Google's generative features (like AI Overviews) prioritize content that shows clear expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness (E-E-A-T).
*   **Structured Data is Mandatory**: Use JSON-LD (Schema.org) to define entities (Person, Service, Organization). This helps AI "understand" who you are and what you do without having to guess from text.
*   **Direct Answers**: AI search favors content that answers specific user queries directly. Use FAQ schemas and clear heading hierarchies.
*   **Citation Readiness**: AI models cite sources. To be cited, your content should be unique, technically accessible, and logically structured.

## 2. Using Generative AI Content
Google does not penalize AI-generated content *if* it provides value.

*   **Quality Over Origin**: Google's systems reward high-quality content, however it is produced.
*   **Avoid "AI-Spam"**: Content created primarily to manipulate search rankings (without providing new information) is flagged as spam.
*   **Transparency**: If AI is used to assist, ensure a human expert reviews and adds unique "Experience" (the 'E' in E-E-A-T) that an AI cannot replicate.

## 3. Core Search Fundamentals
The foundation must be solid for any advanced strategy to work.

*   **Crawlability**: Ensure `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` are properly configured.
*   **Mobile-First**: Google crawls with a smartphone bot. The mobile version of your site is the primary version for indexing.
*   **HTTPS & Security**: A non-secure site will be de-prioritized.
*   **Core Web Vitals (CWV)**:
    *   **LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)**: Loading performance (< 2.5s).
    *   **INP (Interaction to Next Paint)**: Responsiveness (< 200ms).
    *   **CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)**: Visual stability (< 0.1).

## 4. Developer-Specific SEO
*   **Semantic HTML**: Use `<header>`, `<footer>`, `<main>`, `<article>`, and `<h1>`-`<h6>` correctly.
*   **Metadata**:
    *   **Titles**: Should be descriptive and match the user intent.
    *   **Meta Descriptions**: High-impact summaries that encourage clicks (CTR).
*   **Image Optimization**: Use `alt` text, proper dimensions, and modern formats (WebP/AVIF).
*   **Canonical Tags**: Prevent duplicate content issues by explicitly stating the primary URL.

## 5. Ranking "Number 1" Strategy (2026)
1.  **Niche Authority**: Instead of ranking for "WordPress", rank for "WordPress Emergency Recovery" or "WordPress Performance for Founders".
2.  **Entity Linking**: Link your site to your LinkedIn, Upwork, and social profiles via `sameAs` schema to build a "Knowledge Graph" entry.
3.  **CTR Optimization**: Test different titles and descriptions to see which gets more clicks for the same impressions.
4.  **Freshness**: Regularly update your blog or portfolio with "Experience-led" case studies.
